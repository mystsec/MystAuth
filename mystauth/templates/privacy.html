  1. About this policy

This privacy policy explains how the Myst Auth login portal and the websites of domain mystauth.com (“we”) collect and process information from users of Myst Auth’s services. Myst Auth’s services are including, but not limited to, the Myst Auth application, the Myst Auth API, the website and documentation located at mystauth.com, and all other subdomains of mystauth.com.

To the extent that we have links to other websites, please note that we do not own, operate, or control them, and they have their own controlling privacy policies that you should review.

  1. What data we collect

        

Personal data:

  • When you sign up through the Myst Auth login portal, we collect and store the unique username you set in order to authenticate you for our own applications and on behalf of third parties who implement our services.
  • If you are a developer using the Myst Auth API, we may also collect your email and application domain name (should you choose to provide these) so that you can use our services.

     Non-personal data:

  • We may store usage count data and non-identifiable (anonymous) interaction data in order to understand how to best improve our services.

We define “personal data” here as information which could be linked or is linked to you or any other identified individual.

  1. How we use your data

We use your unique username in order to securely authenticate your account. We use aggregate usage and engagement data to improve our services. We use developer contact and domain information to secure and enable developers to implement our services.

  1. How we share your data

We do not and we will not sell your personal data. We will only ever share your data collected if we are legally compelled to. We may share data if it is in an anonymized and aggregate form (such as number of users, etc.) and does not identify any specific individual.

  1. How we protect your data

We have implemented reasonable technical, physical and administrative safeguards to help protect all information from unlawful use and unauthorized disclosure. All our workers are required to follow these established safeguards.

  1. How we use cookies and other technologies

Cookies are small text files that are stored on browsers or devices by websites, apps, online media and advertisements. We uses cookies and similar technologies for purposes such as:

  • authenticating users
  • securing the authentication process
  • remembering user preferences and settings

  1. How you can manage your data

If you wish to access, modify, or destroy your data or you have any other questions regarding this policy or our data practices, please ask us by emailing privacy@mystauth.com.

  1. Children

We created Myst Auth for the exclusive use of users 13 years of age and older. We don't knowingly collect or solicit personal information from children. If you are a child under 13, please do not attempt to use Myst Auth’s products or send any personal information to us.

  1. Changes to this policy

We will continue to update our policies and practices as needed. We will notify you of any changes to our Privacy Statement by posting any changes here. If we do, you'll see that the date at the top of this Privacy Statement has changed.

If you have questions or concerns regarding this policy, please contact our Data Protection Officer by emailing privacy@mystauth.com.
